## What surprised us

- **The §-numbering system created naming friction that the plan did not anticipate.** When step IDs inside the plan were also written as `§1`–`§11`, they collided visually with the §-tree's plan IDs (`§1.1`, `§1.2`). Renaming to `Step 1`–`Step 11` was a free fix in retrospect, but it surfaced only when the user pointed out the §-system was being ignored — it would have been a slow source of confusion otherwise.
- **The provenance rule (AI cannot write `decided-by`/`approved-by`) created an interesting tension** with the user's instruction to "umsetze" the plan. The implementation proceeded *without* approval recorded, on the basis that verbal user instruction is the approval action — but the formal `/approve §1.1` step is still owed. This is the framework working as designed, even if it feels procedural.
- **Writing the Challenger output for §1.1 was the most useful exercise of the whole plan.** The "strongest counter-argument" forced a concession that the motivation came from one critique, not observed user pain. That admission is now load-bearing for §1.2's design — if §1.2 cannot find a real user who will fill these fields, §1.1 should be reconsidered.

## What we now believe

- The Thinking & Learning layer can coexist with the execution lifecycle. The risk is not *technical compatibility* but *adoption friction*.
- The Gatekeeper / Plan Proposal mechanism is the highest-leverage idea in §1.1 because it converts "out of scope" from a loss into a queue. But the value is conditional on humans actually triaging the queue — which the auto-defer rule alone may not be enough to ensure.
- The Challenger role's *durability* is the single biggest open question. It works for n=1; it must be re-evaluated by reading 3–5 future Challenger outputs back-to-back.

## What we still do not know

- Whether average time-to-G2 actually grows >3× on consumer repos (the kill criterion fires here)
- Whether human reviewers will actually `/promote` and `/reject` proposals, or let the folder rot
- Whether the new `evidence:` block produces meaningful entries or boilerplate
- Whether `exploration-mode: A` is ever used in practice, or whether teams skip straight to `B` and treat A as overhead

These are all open hypotheses for **§1.2 — Consumer Rollout** (the next plan in the chapter).
