Short, abstract-style notes on the tools I build and the workflow I use to build them. One idea per paper.

This page collects short, paper-style notes — not finished essays, not product launches. Each one proposes a single idea, sketches its motivation, and shows the smallest concrete artefact that would make the idea real.

Some ideas mature into Lab experiments or land in the framework. Most stay here as drafts: an abstract, a thought, a half-built proof. The point is not to ship — the point is to think out loud, in public, in a form that can be cited and refuted.

  2. 002 PPR-002

    The Challenger Role: Building Adversarial Self-Critique into a Single-Author Workflow Outline

    chevp · planned · 4 min read

    A single-author project has no peer review by default. The framework's Challenger role manufactures one: before every G2, the AI must produce three failure modes, two alternatives, the strongest counter-argument, and a product-coherence check. This paper argues that the cost of a forced critique is small — and that the alternative is sunken cost dressed up as conviction.

  3. 003 PPR-003

    Decisions are Clickable, not Typed: A UI Constraint for Human–AI Workflows Outline

    chevp · planned · 3 min read

    Free-form prose ("shall we proceed?") is the wrong UI for a forking decision. This paper proposes a single rule: every discrete choice in an AI-driven workflow must be presented as a clickable question with 2–4 labelled options plus a free-text fallback. We sketch the contract, the failure modes it eliminates, and where it does not apply.
